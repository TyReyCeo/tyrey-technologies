## Weakness Map

**Three weakest dimensions: Revenue (2), Capital (2), Product (3)**

These three dimensions form a reinforcing failure loop that directly undermines the stated objective of raising capital. Weak Product (unproven differentiation, likely pre-MVP) prevents customers from converting to paid usage, which suppresses Revenue (near-zero monetization proof). The absence of revenue traction then weakens the Capital position, since investors price risk based on evidence of demand — without revenue proof, the company must raise on narrative alone, at a worse valuation, with less leverage, and shorter effective runway. This loop also compounds with the weak Customer dimension (3): without validated pain and design partners, Product cannot be sharpened, which stalls Revenue further, which strains Capital again. Breaking this loop requires starting at Customer/Product, not at Capital directly — fundraising activity alone will not fix the underlying evidence gap.

## Priority Actions

1. **Validate customer pain and convert design partners into paying pilots.**
   *Rationale:* This is the root fix for the Product→Revenue→Capital loop; without it, any raise is narrative-only.
   *30-day success measure:* 15+ discovery interviews completed and at least 1 paid pilot signed.

2. **Build and ship a focused MVP addressing one validated workflow.**
   *Rationale:* Converts vision and market opportunity into demonstrable, fundable proof.
   *30-day success measure:* Working MVP demoed live to 5+ prospects with documented feedback.

3. **Construct a credible financial model and bridge-funding plan.**
   *Rationale:* Directly addresses the Capital weakness and gives investors a concrete runway/use-of-funds story rather than an open-ended ask.
   *30-day success measure:* 18-month financial model and bridge financing term sheet (or committed interest) in hand.
